I am a Postdoctoral Associate at **Yale University**, where I work with Prof. Tianyu Zhu. I develop scalable computational methods and software for quantum many-body physics, with a particular focus on strongly correlated electrons. My work brings together quantum chemistry, many-body theory, scientific machine learning, and high-performance computing.

My current research centers on **transcorrelated electronic-structure theory**, **neural quantum states**, and **quantum embedding**. I lead the development of **PyTC**, a JAX-based framework for transcorrelated calculations. Recent efforts include low-rank interpolative factorizations for transcorrelated Hamiltonians and scalable methods for periodic electronic-structure calculations, with an emphasis on numerical reliability, transparent accuracy tests, and reproducible high-performance implementations.

Before joining Yale, I held postdoctoral research positions at **Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich** and the **California Institute of Technology**, and worked at the **Max Planck Institute for Solid State Research**. I received my PhD in quantum chemistry through the International Max Planck Research School for Condensed Matter Science, working with Prof. Ali Alavi and Prof. Andreas Grüneis. I also served as an external scientific consultant at **ByteDance**, advising on machine-learning research for quantum chemistry.

I am interested in collaborations at the intersection of electronic-structure theory, machine learning, and scientific computing, especially where new algorithms and modern accelerator hardware can make accurate many-body calculations more practical.
